Tips

Figure out what you want to measure. For example:

  • determine power consumption of various devices/circuites
  • identify vampire devices
  • correlate power consumption with temperatures
  • correlate power consumption with occupancy

Create a map of the system. Identify and label each circuit.

Be consistent with the wiring to ensure correct polarity. Polarity is determined by a number of things:

  • location of load relative to breaker
  • single leg 110V circuit vs two legs of 220V circuit
  • orientation of the CT
  • location of CT leads
  • polarity setting within the GEM

Determine how much data you need to store. Determine how much data you can store.

btmon works only with Python 2, not Python 3.

- create a map. circuit/channel/label - label the wires - polarity. be consistent with wiring. options to change polarity later - geometry, leads, gem polarity setting per channel, btmon - figure out what you want to measure. here are typical scenarios: - power on major energy consumers - vampire devices - exterior temperature, interior temperature, heat/cool equipment power, tstat - how much data can you store? - decide on a configuration. here are a few typical: - router that uploads to servers - everything on a router (asus n16 running tomato with an external hd) - everything on a dedicated computer (nimbus with flash drive) - view on cell phone, tablet, computer developer issues - counter wraparound - counter reset - polarity - differential versus cumulative calculations - what to store (diff vs cumulative) - how often to sample, how many samples to store, how often to upload - ability to read all settings, change all settings
